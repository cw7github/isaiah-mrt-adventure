## DETAILED STATION ANALYSIS

### V1: Meaning From Context and Word Parts
**Standards:** L.1.4, L.1.4.A (context), L.1.4.B (affixes), L.1.4.C (root words)

#### ORIGINAL ISSUES FOUND:

**1. VOCABULARY PEDAGOGY - CRITICAL GAPS**
- ❌ Context clues limited to explicit definitions ("Playful means it likes to play")
- ❌ No inference from context (students didn't have to think)
- ❌ Prefix "un-" used but NEVER taught what it means
- ❌ Suffix "-ing" used but NEVER taught what it means
- ⚠️ Poor passage selection ("I use to to find meaning" - vague, not connected to word)

**2. SKILL TRANSFER - WEAK**
- ❌ Students couldn't apply prefix/suffix knowledge (meanings never taught)
- ❌ Context clue strategy limited to explicit definitions
- ✅ Root word strategy was clear and transferable

**3. QUESTION QUALITY - MIXED**
- ✅ Good: "What is the root word in jumping?"
- ❌ Poor: "What is the root word in unhappy?" (passage doesn't contain word)
- ❌ Missing: No questions testing prefix/suffix MEANING
- ❌ Missing: No application to new words

**4. COVERAGE ASSESSMENT:**
- ✅ L.1.4.A: Partial (only explicit definitions, not inference)
- ❌ L.1.4.B: FAILED (prefixes/suffixes mentioned but meanings never taught)
- ✅ L.1.4.C: PASSED (root words taught clearly)

#### FIXES APPLIED:

**Pedagogy Improvements:**
1. Added **inference context clue**: "The playful puppy runs and jumps all day" → infer playful from actions
2. Added **explicit prefix teaching**: "Un at the start means NOT" with happy/unhappy comparison
3. Added **explicit suffix teaching**: "The ending ing means doing it now" with jump/jumping
4. Added **root word family**: "help, helping, helped, helper" showing pattern

**New Questions Added:**
- Q1: Context inference (playful from puppy's actions)
- Q2: **Prefix meaning** - "What does un mean in unhappy?" → "not"
- Q3: **Suffix meaning** - "What does ing mean in jumping?" → "doing it now"
- Q4: Root word identification (helper → help)
- Q5: **Application** - Use context + prefix to figure out "redo"

**Result:** NOW FULLY COVERS L.1.4.A, L.1.4.B, L.1.4.C with explicit, transferable instruction

**3. QUESTION QUALITY - MIXED**

- ⚠️ "Which word is the strongest?" (gigantic) - only 1 adjective question
- ❌ Missing: Ordering tasks, weakest vs strongest comparisons

**4. COVERAGE ASSESSMENT:**

**This was the MOST CRITICAL gap** - missing entire standard.

#### FIXES APPLIED:

**Complete Adjective Instruction Added:**
1. Read page 1: "Tiny, big, and gigantic all mean large" (introduction)
2. Read page 2: "Tiny is smallest. Big is larger. Gigantic is the biggest" (ordering)
3. Visual: "Size ladder: tiny → big → gigantic"

**New Questions:**
- Q1: Verb weakest - "Which word means look quickly?" → peek
- Q2: Verb strongest - "Which word means look the longest?" → stare
- Q3: **Adjective weakest** - "Which word means the smallest?" → tiny
- Q4: **Adjective strongest** - "Which word means the biggest?" → gigantic
- Q5: **Ordering task** - "Put these in order from smallest to biggest" → tiny, big, gigantic

**Updated checklistTargets:** Added "L.1.5.E" explicitly

**Result:** NOW FULLY COVERS both L.1.5.D (verbs) AND L.1.5.E (adjectives) with parallel instruction

## SKILL TRANSFER ASSESSMENT

### Context Clues Strategy (L.1.4.A):
- ✅ **Explicit teaching:** "Use the sentence to figure out what playful means"
- ✅ **Multiple types:** Definition ("means...") + Inference (puppy's actions)
- ✅ **Application:** Students use strategy with new word (redo)
- ✅ **Transferability:** HIGH - strategy works for any unknown word

### Affix Strategy (L.1.4.B):
- ✅ **Explicit teaching:** "Un at the start means NOT" / "ing means doing it now"
- ✅ **Pattern recognition:** Multiple examples (unhappy, redo, jumping)
- ✅ **Meaning-focused:** Not just identification, but understanding what affixes DO
- ✅ **Transferability:** HIGH - students can apply to new prefixes/suffixes

### Root Word Strategy (L.1.4.C):
- ✅ **Explicit teaching:** "Find the smaller word inside"
- ✅ **Word families:** help, helping, helped, helper
- ✅ **Visual support:** Root word highlighted in variations
- ✅ **Transferability:** HIGH - strategy works for any inflected/derived word

### Category Strategy (L.1.5.A/B):
- ✅ **Explicit teaching:** "Say why a word belongs in a group"
- ✅ **Multiple categories:** Colors, Foods, Animals (expandable)
- ✅ **Explanation required:** Not just sorting, but justifying
- ✅ **Transferability:** HIGH - strategy works for any category system

### Real-life Connection (L.1.5.C):
- ✅ **Pattern demonstrated:** Meaning → Real context → Examples
- ✅ **Multiple words:** cozy (places), curious (actions)
- ✅ **Concrete examples:** Bed with blanket, asking questions
- ✅ **Transferability:** MEDIUM-HIGH - works best with concrete, experiential words

### Shades of Meaning (L.1.5.D/E):

- ✅ **Both types:** Verbs (peek→look→stare) AND Adjectives (tiny→big→gigantic)
