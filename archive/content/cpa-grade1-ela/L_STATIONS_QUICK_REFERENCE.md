## Key Features of Every Station

### Instructional Components
- **Read Pages:** 1-4 teaching pages with clear examples
- **Reading Tips:** Scaffolding hints for comprehension
- **Target Words:** Vocabulary highlighted in each lesson
- **Visual Supports:** Icons and emojis throughout

### Assessment Components
- **Questions:** 3-9 multiple-choice questions per station
- **Answer Choices:** Always 3 options (optimal for grade 1)
- **Context Passages:** Every question includes supporting text
- **Hints:** Comprehension support without giving away answers
- **Success Messages:** Immediate positive feedback

### Engagement Features
- **Interactive Menus:** Student choice elements
- **Stickers:** Collectible rewards for completion
- **Themed Content:** Coherent topics within stations
- **Age-Appropriate:** Vocabulary and concepts for 6-7 year-olds

## Usage Tips for Educators

### Before the Lesson
- Review the station's standards and learning targets
- Preview read pages to understand instructional approach
- Note any vocabulary students may need pre-teaching

### During the Lesson
- Have students read instructional pages independently or with support
- Use menu pages as discussion prompts
- Encourage use of hints before answering questions
- Celebrate success messages together

### After the Lesson
- Check completion via checklistTargets
- Reinforce concepts with additional practice if needed
- Connect learning to students' writing and speaking

### Differentiation
- **Advanced learners:** Ask to explain why wrong answers are incorrect
- **Struggling learners:** Review instructional pages before questions
- **ELL students:** Pre-teach target words, use visuals heavily
- **All students:** Encourage explaining their thinking process
